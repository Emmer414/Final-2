FTC





FTC is a nation-wide robotics program designed for students in grades 7-12 to compete head to head. Teams are responsible for designing, building, and programming their robots to compete in an alliance format against other teams.

FTC is very beneficial to members of Team Fusion and other robotics teams in several ways. For one, it promotes an interest and an understanding in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. Two, it helps students develop “coopertition,” which is cooperation and sportsmanship between competing teams. It helps create a positive atmosphere in the face of competition, and teams are able to develop friendly rivalries along the way. Three, it gives the teams a chance to show off their abilities and display gracious professionalism and maturity. This helps the teams “keep their cool” if they lose the competition.

This "small" competition requires students to follow the Design process through and through like the FRC competition. However, the FTC competition presents its own challenges as teams are restricted to an 18-inch cube size.

To find out more, check out the FTC page on the official FIRST website here.
